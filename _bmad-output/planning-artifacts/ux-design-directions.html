kano — Analysis-page design directions

Three compositional variations for the defining interaction (the PM's first scan of the analysis page). Shared across all three: Tixeo Vuetify theme, Kano category palette, same illustrative dataset (6 features, 12 responses, project pinned to version 2). Different: per-feature layout, tie-state rendering, and reading flow.

Direction 1 — Card-Grid

Each feature is a 50%-width card in a 2-column grid. The dominant category name + percentage sits at the top of each card in large type; the stacked bar underneath is the visual confirmation of the already-resolved claim. Inspired by Linear Insights and Stripe Dashboard headline cards.

StrengthsScan-first hierarchy: the 32px percentage is the first thing read per feature. Screenshot-friendly unit — one card is a clean quote-copy target. Tie state gets dedicated breathing room.
TradeoffsVertical real estate: 20 features require substantial scrolling. The 2-column composition can read "dashboard-like" (multiple widgets), slightly at odds with Principle 4 ("artifact, not dashboard"). Per-feature weight is uniform — a Must-have 71% and a Questionable 22% look equally visually prominent.

Direction 2 — Table-Row

Each feature is a row in a column-aligned table. Dominant percentage, category badge, stacked bar, and response count on a single horizontal line. Inspired by Linear issue lists and Linear/GitHub insights tables.

StrengthsDense scan: 20 features fit without significant scrolling. Column alignment makes cross-feature comparison trivial — "which features are Must-have?" reads at a glance. Most artifact-like: screenshots well at any zoom; consistent with the Linear-table pattern already locked for PM home (Sub-problem 2).
TradeoffsLess breathing room for tie-state richness (compensated by badges in the dominant column). Less emotionally "weighty" per-feature — a Must-have 71% is just another row rather than a headline-card moment.

Direction 3 — Split-View

Feature list on the left with mini-bars; selected-feature detail panel on the right with rich tie-state rendering, big stacked bar, and per-category legend. Inspired by Apple Mail / iA Writer-style master-detail.

StrengthsDetail-rich per feature: ties get maximum breathing room, full category legend with counts. Scales to long feature lists without layout pressure. Exploration-oriented.
TradeoffsNot an artifact — the screenshot-worthy unit is the detail panel alone, not the full page. Forces an interaction (pick a feature) to read each claim, breaking the "scan = reading" mechanic. Structurally at odds with Principle 4 ("the analysis page is the product's artifact, not its dashboard"). Recommend rejecting unless exploration genuinely matters more than quotability.

Facilitator recommendation

Direction 2 (Table-Row) is the closest match to the locked design principles: most artifact-like (screenshots well at any zoom, cross-feature comparison trivial), densest (supports growth to 20-feature polls without scroll pain), and consistent with the Linear-table pattern already committed for the PM home surface (Sub-problem 2 in the inspiration analysis).

Direction 1 (Card-Grid) is a strong fallback if per-feature breathing room and a weightier tie-state presentation matter more than density. Good hybrid candidate if Direction 2's tie-state treatment turns out to feel cramped during prototyping.

Direction 3 (Split-View) is at structural odds with Principle 4 ("artifact, not dashboard") — recommend rejecting unless cross-feature exploration genuinely becomes a v1 requirement.
